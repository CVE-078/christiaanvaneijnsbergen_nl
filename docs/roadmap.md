# Pulse, Product Roadmap

## Product vision & positioning

**Pulse is an adaptive strength coach that works on any device.** Build a plan, track your lifts, and it adapts when life gets in the way so you keep progressing for years, not weeks. The periodization, adherence, ramp-back, and deload logic are the mechanism behind that promise, not the pitch.

**The moat:**
- Completion-paced programs (you advance by finishing cycles, not by the calendar).
- Ramp-back weeks after a layoff, auto-deload on stalled lifts, adherence awareness (on-track / behind / lapsed).
- Deterministic, equipment-aware generation that is genuinely strong for home-gym, dumbbell-only, and travel setups.
- Cross-device and offline-first (any browser, no ecosystem lock-in), the gap Apple-only apps like Liftin cannot fill.

**Target user:** the training-first lifter (strength and hypertrophy), at home or in a gym, who wants structure without a spreadsheet. Not a social network, not a running / CrossFit / rehab app, not an AI chatbot. Expand to adjacent users (for example movement restrictions) through the generation engine, never by diluting the core.

**Design principle:** simple surface, ignorable intelligence. The app carries a lot of smarts; the UI stays calm and lets users ignore most of it until it matters.

**Not building** (focus guards, all evaluated): AI chatbot, LLM-generated workouts, heavy fatigue modeling, goal prediction, native apps and wearables until real traction (the native *how* is settled and the *whether* is held open under "Native client, deferred direction" below).

**Social -- hard-out permanently:** global leaderboards, competitive rankings, public follower counts, feed-first home screen (Train is always the home screen), viral challenges, engagement farming mechanics, likes or reactions on body or progress photos, network/comparison achievements (self-referential milestones -- your own PRs / streaks / volume bests -- are on-vision; only the comparative kind stays hard-out), DMs or in-app messaging, creator economy or paid content. (Achievements split into two lanes: self-referential milestones are post-launch-OK and on-vision; only the network/social kind stays hard-out, since it pulls toward the social-network anti-goal.)

**Social -- deferred, not ruled out:** lightweight training-community features (routine link sharing, friend activity, private accountability groups, public routine pages) are a documented long-term direction, not a near-term build. The distinction is "not social-first", not "not social". See "Social direction, deferred" below.

## Launch floor (pre-public)

The data-safety and account-lifecycle work that gates a public launch. These are not features, they are the floor: "don't lose or leak user data, don't trap the user." Features wait behind them. Sourced from the multi-app review sweep (Liftin', Runna), whose loudest one-star drivers were sync data-loss and billing opacity, cross-checked against the code. Nothing here jumps the floor or the validation gate; the action is verifying these failure modes are handled before strangers arrive.

- **Auth + billing lifecycle (the long pole).** Public launch needs the full account lifecycle built together: signup / email verify / password reset / account delete (no signup route exists in code yet). The sweep extends this to **billing specifically**, cancellation difficulty, opaque billing, and charges after account deletion are the #1 complaint driver across Runna reviews. So the paid-tier seam must include a clean, honest cancel / refund / billing-transparency path built as carefully as signup, plus a real support contact (not a bot). **Positioning note (not a build):** a one-time / lifetime payment option is a real weapon against subscription fatigue and fits Pulse's no-dark-patterns stance, worth considering when designing the tier, or at minimum keep subscription terms transparent. **Cross-device session continuity (security boundary):** the safe version is already the default, Supabase persists an authenticated session per device (the SSR cookie session the middleware refreshes), so "logged in everywhere" needs no new mechanism. If you want lower sign-in friction, do it through owner-bound standard flows: single-use, short-TTL magic-link / OTP sign-in delivered to the account's own email, or a QR device-pairing approval from an already-authed device (the WhatsApp-Web model). **Never a link that *carries* authentication**, a forwarded or shared link that auto-logs-in whoever holds it is an account-takeover path, and stays out.

- **Accessibility (a11y) audit, sequenced AFTER the auth lifecycle, not before.** A full a11y sweep, not yet done. The code audit (2026-06-06) surfaced one symptom, the guided `WorkoutModeScreen` overlay missing `role="dialog"`/`aria-modal` and some unlabeled icon-buttons (audit Q8), and one flagged modal almost always means the same patterns recur elsewhere, so this needs a dedicated pass, not a spot-fix. Treated as a launch-quality **floor, not deferred**, because the launch audience is EU/NL, where the European Accessibility Act (in force 2025) makes basic digital accessibility a legal consideration, not only a quality one. **Triage like the general audit:** fix the floor (WCAG 2.1 A + AA essentials, keyboard operability, focus management on modals/overlays, accessible names, semantic structure, contrast) before launch; file richer ARIA/live-region/reduced-motion as opportunistic Bucket 2; drop/defer the AAA-and-edge tail. **Highest-priority surface: the core logging loop** (`ExerciseCard`, `SetLogger`, guided `WorkoutModeScreen`), a keyboard or screen-reader user must be able to log a set start-to-finish. **Pulse-specific checks the standard sweep would miss:** (1) verify contrast against **every accent preset** (coral/emerald/sky/violet/amber/rose), not just default coral, since a user-picked accent multiplies the contrast surface by six; (2) part-manual, not tooling-only (automated scans catch ~⅓–½; the manual VoiceOver/keyboard "log a set without looking" core-loop test is the highest-value part); (3) test on a real mobile device with VoiceOver (iOS) / TalkBack (Android) + touch-target sizing, since installed-PWA mobile is the primary surface. **Preliminary contrast already computed** (so the eventual sweep starts from data): the bright accent-as-foreground passes AA on the dark theme for all six presets (6.4–11.4) and dark-label-on-accent buttons pass, so that fear is defused; the real AA gaps are accent-independent, `pulse-muted` body text fails the 4.5 floor on bg/surface/surface-2 (4.21/3.89/3.61, large-text-only) and the dim-accent foregrounds fail on most presets (≈3.3–4.4). Confirms this is a genuine defect to fix, not a theoretical one. **Sequencing rationale:** signup / login / reset / verification are net-new, high-stakes a11y surfaces that don't exist yet (a screen-reader user who can't complete signup never reaches the app); auditing now would miss the most important new front-door surface and force a re-audit. **Exception that jumps the queue:** fix the known Q8 modal issues opportunistically if already inside `WorkoutModeScreen` for the guided-set-row polish (Tier 1 #2), that is the opportunistic-fix discipline, not running the formal sweep early.

- **Operational infrastructure (the "operating it in production" floor).** These are the gaps a feature-focused solo dev overlooks because at two users *you* are the monitoring, support, and error reporting, which silently stops being true at launch. Triage: the first four are floor (regret-launching-without), the rest are confirm-don't-assume.
  - **Error / crash tracking (floor).** Today a production runtime error is invisible until it surfaces as a one-star review ("it glitched and lost my workout", a real Runna/Hevy review pattern). Need production error capture with stack + route context. Sentry (free tier ample at this scale) or PostHog error tracking (one fewer vendor if PostHog is adopted later, see the analytics decision record above). Pairs with the offline-queue dead-letter work, a dead-lettered write should surface to *you* (the `deadLetteredCount` already exists), not just sit in IndexedDB. Launching blind to production errors is launching without a smoke detector.
  - **Database backups + a tested restore (floor, confirm/upgrade).** The product promise is "your training history, for years," so DB loss is existential and a botched restore loses a user forever. Confirm the Supabase plan's backup cadence, recovery window (point-in-time recovery), and *actually test a restore once*. Free-tier Supabase backup retention is limited, if so, this is a reason to upgrade the tier before launch. "There are backups" ≠ "I've restored one."
  - **Rate limiting / abuse protection (floor, build into the auth lifecycle).** No rate limiting on write paths or auth today; fine at two trusted users, not public. Two surfaces matter: auth (signup/login, credential-stuffing, signup spam, verification-email abuse) and the mutation routes (a runaway/buggy client flooding the DB). Needn't be elaborate, Supabase has some built-in auth limiting; a basic per-user/per-IP limit on mutations covers the rest. Build it *into* the auth-lifecycle work rather than bolting it on later.
  - **Production email deliverability (floor, part of the auth lifecycle).** Signup verification, password reset, and transactional email depend on email actually arriving (SPF/DKIM/DMARC, sender reputation, not landing in spam). Supabase's default email sending is dev-grade and rate-limited; production signups likely need a transactional provider (Resend / Postmark / SendGrid) wired to Supabase Auth with domain email auth configured. A reset email that bounces or spam-folders is a locked-out → dead user. Part of the auth-lifecycle build, not separate, it's the part people forget until reset emails fail.
  - **Confirm-don't-assume (lower effort, mostly near-free on Vercel + Supabase):** uptime/health monitoring (a free pinger on a health endpoint, so "is it down" isn't answered by a user); a staging/preview environment with a non-production Supabase so destructive migrations are never tested against the only users' real data (Vercel preview deploys give most of this free); migration safety/rollback discipline (hand-written migrations against prod data get higher-stakes once it's irreplaceable, test against a copy, know how to roll back); CI on push (the full test suite + typecheck running automatically so a regression can't reach prod silently, Vercel can gate deploys on it).
  - **Explicitly NOT needed yet (don't over-build):** CDN beyond Vercel's default, a separate caching layer (Redis), container orchestration, load balancing, multi-region, a dedicated logging pipeline beyond error tracking. All scale infrastructure for a user base that doesn't exist; the Vercel + Supabase stack handles this class of concern precisely so a solo dev doesn't reach for it early.

- **Legal / compliance pages (floor, named deliverable, not just an implied mention).** Privacy policy, terms of service, and a cookie/consent mechanism with a clear data-processing basis. Currently referenced only in passing (the analytics GDPR note, implied in the auth lifecycle) but not owned as a concrete item. Required floor for an EU/NL launch handling health-adjacent data (bodyweight, measurements, training history), same tier as account deletion. Not a big build, but a GDPR-compliant privacy policy for health-adjacent data is not a fill-in-the-blank template, and it's the kind of task that's nobody's until it blocks launch day, so name it now. Shares the consent surface with the analytics decision record above. **Caveat (not lawyer-able from here):** these roadmap items mean "make sure this is handled," not "this is now legally sufficient", for health-adjacent EU body data, a one-time actual legal check (even a single consultation on the privacy policy + consent basis) is worth more than any roadmap wording. Treat the obligations named here as the checklist, not the clearance.
- **Incident + rollback plan (floor, lightweight, extends migration safety).** The connective tissue between backups and error tracking: the deliberate answer to "what do I do when production breaks with real users," not a heavy runbook (solo dev). Three concrete cases: (1) roll back a bad deploy (Vercel is ~one click, confirm the procedure is known); (2) roll back a bad *migration*, the real one, since migrations are hand-written and applied to prod data, so it is the higher-stakes case and overlaps the migration-safety confirm-item above; (3) a stated tolerance for "app is down while I'm asleep." The value is having thought it through once, before it happens at 2am on live user data.
- **Account data export (floor, GDPR portability, pairs with account deletion).** GDPR's data-portability right. The shipped CSV export partially covers this for workout logs; the action is to confirm whether that satisfies the portability obligation or whether the account-deletion floor item should also carry a full-account export companion (deletion and export are the two halves of "the user controls their data", same surface). Likely small or already mostly covered; confirm sufficiency rather than assume new build.

**Floor already shipped (do not re-audit):** offline-queue cross-user safety (per-user replay, never dropped/leaked) **and poison-pill / dead-letter hardening** (a permanently-failing write no longer stalls the queue; auth-expiry stays transient), live RLS audit (all user-data tables owner-scoped in prod), decimal-comma input parsing, rest-timer-survives-lock reliability, and the **2026-06-06 code/security audit** (server-action authz, input validation, secrets, CSP all verified, see `docs/audits/`). See Shipped.

**Still open on the floor:** the auth + billing lifecycle (above), the a11y audit (below, sequenced after it), the **operational-infrastructure** items above (error tracking + tested backups stand alone; rate-limiting + email deliverability fold into the auth lifecycle), the **legal/compliance pages** (privacy policy + ToS + consent) and **incident/rollback plan**, confirming the CSV export covers GDPR portability, and capturing the live schema in version control (a reproducibility gate flagged by the audit, RLS is enabled and prod-verified, but not all of it is in `docs/migrations`: some RLS policy bodies, plus the **base DDL for the earliest tables** is missing, e.g. `bodyweight_logs` has no `CREATE TABLE` in any migration, and the `profiles.unit` column, used in `queries.ts`, was never created by a tracked migration. Reconstruct a baseline schema dump so the DB is reproducible from VCS).

### Routine generation v2, engine direction

A full code-grounded architecture audit (2026-06-06; both AI reviews converged) is captured in `docs/superpowers/designs/2026-06-06-00-30-19-generation-engine-v2-audit.md`. Conclusion: **the generator is already well-architected and should not be rewritten. The next bottleneck is data richness, not the algorithm.** The shipped pipeline already is the layered model both reviews proposed: profile → training archetype (`STYLES` by day count) → movement-pattern slots (`EMPHASES`) → equipment-filtered selection → progression.

Single most important finding: generation is purely **slot-first** (fill session slots, weekly volume emerges). `VOLUME_TARGETS` / `priorityAdjustedTargets` exist but feed only the Progress / recovery analytics, never generation, and the two systems speak different vocabularies (15 `MovementPattern`s vs 10 `ExerciseCategory`s) with no bridge. That missing bridge is the foundation everything else depends on.

Phased plan (do in order, foundations before features; "build trust before more intelligence"). A research-consensus draft of the Phase 0 content (muscle map, metadata, contraindications, volume landmarks, training-style definitions, strength standards, adaptation copy), with reconciliation notes against Pulse's actual vocabulary, is captured in `docs/superpowers/designs/2026-06-06-00-54-52-phase0-source-material.md`.

Expand to quad/ham + delt splits only if specialization later demands it.** Expand exercise metadata (primary/secondary muscles into the generation path + backfill, add `fatigue_cost`, `unilateral`, `joint_stress`, `difficulty`, and a substitution class for equivalence grouping); add `session_id` + a workout date to `set_logs` so skip detection / adherence / behavior learning become trustworthy. **Define a standard `DecisionEvent` schema** (`type` = ramp_back / deload / progression / swap; plus `trigger`, `affected_area`, `magnitude`, `confidence`) and log every adaptive event through it, ramp-back already persists in `program_adjustments`, but auto-deload and progression do not, so the Phase 2 timeline is a computable system log, not a cosmetic journal. Optionally tune `VOLUME_TARGETS` toward the research landmarks (the draft's MAV runs higher than Pulse's current bands for back / glutes / shoulders). **(Status, 2026-06-06: Phase 0 COMPLETE. ### Product decisions & principles (2026-06-06)

- **Gender is no longer a programming input, and is now fully optional.** Its only generation role today is `genderDefault` (female to glutes priority), a weak proxy. Replace it with a direct "what do you want to prioritize?" question (the deferred explicit-priority step). **Gender no longer gates the Strength Score (shipped 2026-06-06):** with a gender set it uses the gender-specific standards; without one it scores against a neutral gender-midpoint standard (`NEUTRAL_STANDARDS` / `standardsFor`) and flags the result `approximate`, so the user can skip the question and still get a number, refined later by setting gender. Net: gender stays for analytics + score precision, stops guessing the program, and is never required. **Science-validation candidate:** the midpoint as the "unspecified" baseline is a defensible engineering choice, not a researched standard, worth a pass through the Claude.ai loop if it matters.
- **Generate fast, refine later (onboarding-friction guard).** Every Phase 1 preference input is another onboarding step on top of the existing 6-7. Keep onboarding minimal and generate immediately, then expose style / restrictions / equipment-preference / variety as an optional post-generation "refine your plan" step (Profile or a tune-it screen), not as new required onboarding questions. Decide this before building Phase 1 so the funnel does not bloat.
- **Templates vs generation, pick a role.** 17 hand-authored templates and the generator coexist, and `recommendTemplate` is now largely vestigial, so two systems are drifting. Decide deliberately: either templates stay as the "named program I trust" anchor (5/3/1, PPL) with a distinct purpose, or they fold into generation over time. Otherwise they are maintenance debt. This is a decision to make before extending either path, not a feature.
- **Three-layer architecture (keep the layers separate).** Movement layer (the 15 `MovementPattern`s + equipment + slots) is the generation control system; Exercise layer (per-exercise muscles / fatigue / substitution class) is the mapping; Outcome layer (per-muscle volume, progression, adherence, recovery, the 10 categories) is reporting. **The outcome layer must never feed generation directly, only through the mapping layer.** This is already true (analytics never touch the generator) and must stay true when volume-first generation arrives: the planner reads targets through the bridge, it does not import the analytics. It is also what keeps `MovementPattern` (control) and the 10 categories (reporting) from collapsing into one another. The real moat this protects is traceable, explainable adaptation: every program change answers what changed, why, which rule fired, and what happens next.

### Infrastructure, i18n foundation (next-intl)

Deliberate infrastructure, chosen early. **Not a launch blocker, and must not block or delay the auth-lifecycle or comma-parsing floor work**, it runs as a parallel track on an isolated branch (large diff expected; pure extraction, review carefully). The goal: adding a second language later is a drop-in message file with zero refactor of the string layer, so we set up a real, App-Router-native i18n library now rather than a hand-rolled JSON convention that would itself need migrating later (that would defeat the purpose).

- **Library: `next-intl`**, built for Next.js 15 App Router + React Server Components, native ICU MessageFormat. Chosen over react-i18next (client-first, awkward in RSC), next-i18next (Pages-Router era), and Lingui / Paraglide (fine but not materially better here, less App-Router-native).
- **Mode: without i18n routing.** No `[locale]` route segment and no next-intl routing middleware. Configure `getRequestConfig`, wrap the tree with `NextIntlClientProvider` (in `PulseLayout`), and use `getTranslations` (server) / `useTranslations` (client). This avoids restructuring every route under `app/[locale]/` and any collision with the existing `/pulse` Supabase-session middleware (`src/middleware.ts`); no CSP change. A real second locale later is a small, localized add (detection + switcher), not a re-extraction.
- **Structure:** `messages/en/<namespace>.json` composed in `getRequestConfig`; a single `en` locale; no UI switcher. Namespaces by surface: `train`, `progress`, `plan`, `library`, `profile`, `common` (buttons, nav, generic labels), and `coaching` (all adaptive-voice strings: progression targets, stalled / deload copy, ramp-back nudges, priority-tilt rationale).
- **Extraction scope:** move every current user-facing hardcoded string into the right namespace. A move, not a rewrite, English text stays byte-identical.
- **Interpolation contract (strict):** all formatting and unit conversion happen in TS before the value reaches the message layer; pass display-ready named variables (weights arrive already unit-converted). No conversion functions (`toDisplay`) or unit logic inside message files. Conditional clauses use ICU `select` / composed messages, never string concatenation of fragments. Canonical case: `You stalled around {prevWeight} {unit} × {prevReps}, so back off on purpose. Drop to {targetWeight} {unit} × {targetReps}{deloadClause} to reset.`, `deloadClause` is an ICU-conditional message, not a glued string. The just-shipped `deloadTankClause` / `rirClause` (currently TS string-concat in `SetLogger`) are the first conversions and the proof of the pattern.
- **Double-duty strings (cannot use the React hook):** pure lib functions that emit user-facing strings, notably `buildRationale` (a pure function called from a server action, builds the priority-tilt sentence), plus the regen / ramp-back nudge copy, `strength.ts`'s `reason`, and the recommendation reasons. Restructure to return keys / structured data and translate at a layer that has a translator (`getTranslations` in the server action), or pass a translator in. Do not leave them hardcoded.
- **Verify:** rendered app visually and textually identical to before across all views (zero copy changes). Deliverable: a summary of the namespace structure and any strings that were doing double duty or needed special interpolation handling.

### Infrastructure, typed domain vocabulary (magic strings → const-objects/unions, domain-grouped types)

**Bucket 2 (maintainability), file-opportunistic, NOT launch floor.** A refactor with no user-facing payoff; it must not compete with the launch floor or the validation gate, and must not jump them. Do it **domain-by-domain, opportunistically, when already in a domain for feature work**, the same incremental discipline as the i18n extraction above. A one-pass type-reorg is a large diff touching nearly every import, exactly the churn to avoid near the floor (breakage risk when stability matters most). Each domain moved keeps typecheck + the full suite green.

- **Goal:** eliminate scattered hard-coded domain strings (movement patterns, categories, adjustment kinds, biases, etc.) in favor of centralized, typed, single-source-of-truth values, and organize shared type declarations by domain.
- **Group by DOMAIN, not by language construct.** Do **not** create `/types`, `/interfaces`, `/enums` folders, folder-by-construct fights how the code changes (a domain's types/interfaces/values change together, so the split forces every conceptual change across three directories). Instead group shared declarations by domain area under `lib/pulse/types/` (e.g. `exercise.ts`, `generation.ts`, `program.ts`, `adherence.ts`), each holding the types, interfaces, AND domain value-constants for that domain together. One barrel `index.ts` if clean imports help.
- **Do not split `type` vs `interface` into separate locations**, the distinction is a local implementation choice in TypeScript, not an organizing principle; both are shape declarations and belong together.
- **Co-locate vs centralize (decide per type, don't force one bucket):** centralize the cross-cutting shared vocabulary (`MovementPattern`, `ExerciseCategory`, the core domain unions/values used by many modules) in `lib/pulse/types/`; keep module-private types co-located with their module.
- **No TypeScript `enum`.** Use the const-object + derived-union pattern (`as const` + `type Foo = typeof Foo[keyof typeof Foo]`). TS `enum` has runtime quirks and `const enum` has known problems with bundlers / isolated-modules (relevant to the Next/SWC build); the const-object gives the same named-constant ergonomics, autocomplete, and exhaustiveness with none of the footguns. **Reframe the work:** much of the vocabulary already exists as union types (`MovementPattern`, `ExerciseCategory`, `AdjustmentKind`, `Bias`, and now `DecisionEventType`/`DecisionTrigger`), so for most code this is "centralize the canonical values and make scattered inline strings reference the union/const," not "introduce enums", pair each union with a const object of its values where logic needs the runtime values.

### Native client, deferred direction (Expo + react-native-web)

A decision record, not a planned task. This settles **how** Pulse would go native **if** it ever does; **whether** it does is genuinely open and gated behind two gates, not one. Most likely it stays "not yet" for a long time, and that is a fine place for it to stay: the PWA already is the cross-platform app.

- **Unbundle "App Store presence" from "rewrite the client."** They are separate decisions. Store presence is testable cheaply by wrapping the existing PWA with Capacitor plus one genuine native feature (push) to clear Apple's guideline 4.2 (thin web wrappers get rejected). **The wrap tests discovery and trust only, not native feel**, so a positive wrap result is evidence for *staying wrapped*, not for rewriting. Don't let a good wrap justify the expensive bet. **iOS caveat (price it as a small project, not a config flag):** Capacitor renders in WKWebView, which does not reliably run service workers, so the SW-based offline *shell* needs Capacitor's native cache equivalent. The IndexedDB write queue (the data-safety layer) works fine in WKWebView, so offline writes survive the wrap, only the SW piece is re-homed.
- **The traffic motivation argues for the wrap, against the rewrite.** App-store discovery for an unknown solo app in a category owned by Hevy / Strong / Fitbod is effectively zero without paid UA or an existing audience; the store is a conversion-and-trust surface for traffic you drove there, not a discovery channel. That benefit is almost entirely captured by the cheap wrap. The rewrite buys native feel + watch, which is a *different* goal, confirm you actually have it before paying.
- **If the gates are cleared and native feel / watch is genuinely needed: route is Expo + react-native-web** (one codebase to iOS / Android / web), **not native-only RN**, RNW keeps a web target, so it protects the cross-platform moat instead of trading it away. Viable for Pulse specifically (audited): the data layer is already client-SWR (read path ports; supabase-js runs in RN; only the server-action mutations need rework), 14 of 20 `lib/pulse` files are web-independent (the brain ports verbatim), and NativeWind carries the Tailwind tokens. Cost: a full presentation-layer rewrite (App Router, service worker, server actions, all CSS do not port).
- **RNW keeps a web app, so the product stays linkable / usable-in-browser**, shareability is *not* lost (and the app is already behind login today). The real, smaller loss is web-app polish / SEO vs bespoke Next, handled by **keeping the Next marketing + landing site as the real-web SEO surface** (app screens are authed, never needed SEO). Escape hatch if the RNW web app proves too weak for the app itself: Next-for-web + RN-for-native sharing the brain (two UI layers, the maintenance cost RNW avoids), a build-time fork, not a now-decision.
- **Credit the no-install edge to the web target, not to deeplinks.** The indie distribution advantage is the *absence of an install step on the conversion path*: a link opens the running web app with zero friction, while a deeplink to a native app still resolves, for an *uninstalled* prospect, to an App Store download (the conversion cliff). Deeplinks only serve installed-user continuity. So the web target is what preserves cold-prospect conversion. **Do not drift to native-only on the theory "deeplinks handle sharing"**, that drops the no-install path, and deeplinks cannot recover it. Keep the web target and deeplinks are a bonus on top.
- **Knock-on effects on already-queued work:** the monorepo question reopens only at the point of committing to Expo (Expo universal *is* a monorepo: shared `packages/app` consumed by `apps/native` + `apps/web`), informed by two real apps rather than guessed at. The queued standalone-repo extraction should keep `lib/pulse` cleanly importable so it becomes that shared package with no re-extraction. The launch-floor offline-queue poison-pill fix doubles as separating queue *policy* from *storage*, which makes the eventual IndexedDB → SQLite/MMKV swap a swap, not a rewrite.
- **Reopen bar (a real gate, not a formality):** a committed active-user number, **plus** the validation gate cleared (completion-paced adaptive coaching proven to work for real users, native is downstream of the product being worth distributing wider), **plus** specific evidence the PWA itself is the wall: watch-centric lifters refusing to switch, iOS PWA storage eviction, or App Store being the channel you actually need.

### Social direction, deferred (not social-first)

A decision record, not a planned task. Pulse is a training coach, not a social network. This section documents the long-term direction so the distinction between "hard-out permanently" and "deferred, not ruled out" is clear and explicit, and so the right design constraints are captured before any social surface is built. Nothing here is scheduled work.

**The reframe.** "Not social" was always a shorthand for "not social-network-first, not engagement-optimised, not comparison-driven." It was never a permanent ban on features that help people train together, share useful training work, or hold each other accountable. The honest framing is: Pulse is not social-first. The adaptive coach is the product. Social features, if and when they arrive, support training adherence -- they do not replace it.

**The test for any proposed social feature.** Does it make training better, or does it only make the app more sticky? Features that only do the latter are out. A friend seeing your PR is useful accountability. A leaderboard ranking your PR against strangers is engagement farming.

**The four phases, as a documented direction.**

- **Phase 1: Routine link sharing.** A user can share a routine via a URL. The recipient sees the routine's structure and can install it. On install, Pulse adapts the routine to their equipment, session length, and preferences via the existing generation machinery. No social graph required. No account needed to view. No follower mechanics. Closest analogy: an export that someone else can run. This is the smallest useful first step and the foundation for Phase 4. *Reopen bar:* validation gate cleared, real users exist, and routine sharing has a clear observed use case (users asking how to share their program).
- **Phase 2: Friend activity.** Users can add friends (mutual accept required -- no follow-without-consent). Friends see a simple activity stream of training events: workout completed, new PR, program completed, streak milestone, first workout back after a gap. No engagement actions beyond a single congratulate tap (no like counts displayed). Activity is a secondary surface, never the home screen. *Reopen bar:* Phase 1 shipped, enough active users that friend activity feels alive for at least some of them.
- **Phase 3: Private accountability groups.** Small closed groups (2-20 people) for friends, family, or gym partners. The group sees the same Phase 2 activity events scoped to members. No public discovery of groups, no public member lists, no rankings within the group. *Reopen bar:* Phase 2 shipped, evidence that bilateral friend activity isn't sufficient for the accountability use case.
- **Phase 4: Public routine pages.** A user can make a routine public, which generates a browsable page showing the split, exercises, equipment required, program length, and copy count. No public user profile. No follower count. Light signals: copy count (not likes), optional 1-5 rating on install. Users browse without an account; installing requires one. A content moderation plan is required before this ships. *Reopen bar:* Phase 1 shipped, real user base exists, evidence that routine discovery is a meaningful acquisition channel.

**Hard-out list (permanent, not deferred).** Global leaderboards or competitive rankings. Public follower counts displayed anywhere. Feed-first home screen. Viral challenges or streak wars. Likes or reactions on body or progress photos. Network/comparison achievements. DMs or in-app messaging (high moderation burden, low training value). Creator economy or monetised profiles. Follow without mutual consent. Public profiles that surface personal body data.

**Domain model to consider when the time comes (not schema yet).**

- Phase 1: `routines.visibility` ('private' | 'link_only' | 'public'), `routines.share_token` uuid, `routines.slug` text, public route `/r/[share_token]`.
- Phase 2: `user_connections(id, requester_id, addressee_id, status, created_at)` status: pending | accepted | blocked. `activity_events(id, user_id, event_type, payload jsonb, visibility, created_at)` -- event_type: workout_completed | pr_achieved | program_completed | streak_milestone | first_back_after_gap. Visibility: friends_only (default) | group_only | private.
- Phase 3: `groups(id, name, created_by, created_at)`, `group_members(group_id, user_id, role, joined_at)`. Activity events gain optional `group_id` scoping.
- Phase 4: `routine_installs(id, routine_id, installed_by, installed_at)` for copy count. `routine_ratings(id, routine_id, rated_by, rating smallint, created_at)`, one per user per routine.
- Never: a general `followers` table (asymmetric follow moves toward the Twitter model), engagement metrics stored as columns (view counts, like counts -- these optimise for the wrong thing), personal body data joined to any public-facing table.

**Privacy and safety constraints to carry from the start.** Private by default on every new social object. Friend requests require mutual acceptance. GDPR erasure cascades to all social graph data (connections, activity events, group memberships). Account deletion removes all of it. No body photo sharing in any social context. Activity events are friends-only by default, never public by default. Phase 4 moderation plan (routine names and descriptions are user-generated text, even if benign) must exist before public routines ship. No username search / user discovery until Phase 2, and even then prefer invite-by-link over open search.

**What does not change now.** Auth surface, account profiles, visibility settings, and the Train screen are all unchanged. Phase 1 (routine sharing) is the only phase that touches existing surfaces (adding a share button to a routine and a public-facing read-only route). Phases 2-4 each add new surfaces rather than modifying existing ones, which keeps the blast radius of each phase contained.

**Risks.** (1) Social gravity: each phase makes the next easier to justify. The hard-out list above is only useful if it is actively defended at each phase gate, not just written once. (2) Network effects in Phase 2: friend activity needs a cluster of users training simultaneously to feel alive. If the user base is small when Phase 2 ships, the feature will feel empty. Phase 1 has standalone value; Phase 2 does not. (3) Moderation cliff in Phase 4: user-generated text at scale requires a plan before it ships, not after. (4) Auth surface expansion: friend-adding requires user discovery. Username/handles or email-based invites are both new surfaces with non-trivial privacy and abuse implications. Phase 1 avoids this entirely.

### Monetization philosophy, decision record (not a planned task)

A decision record, capturing the principle that should govern pricing once the auth + billing lifecycle (launch floor, above) actually gets built, so choices made under launch pressure don't drift from what the product already promises. The launch-floor bullet above names the *mechanics* (a clean cancel / refund / billing-transparency path, the positioning note that lifetime pricing fits the no-dark-patterns stance); this names the *principle* behind them, so the mechanics have something to answer to.

- **New to this record.** The free / paid line itself (logging, one program, and basic progress free; generation personalization, the Coach Decision Timeline, and deeper analytics as the premium surface is the natural cut, matching what the product is actually for) and the pricing structure (subscription vs lifetime vs hybrid) are product decisions with real consequences, not implementation details to default on. Decide them at the same table as the billing-lifecycle build, not earlier (pricing decided pre-validation calcifies around assumptions the validation block may overturn) and not as an afterthought once the billing code already exists.
- **Reopen bar.** This stays a principle, not a build, until the auth + billing lifecycle item is actually scheduled, that is the moment the free / paid line and the pricing structure stop being a decision record and become a real build decision that needs answering before the first paywall ships.

### Infrastructure, product analytics (PostHog, EU), post-launch

A backlog decision record, not a launch-floor item. Instruments the **public** phase, once there are strangers to learn from; it does **not** gate launch and must not delay it.

- **Tool: PostHog, EU Cloud region.** Chosen over Vercel Analytics / GA because the need is **product analytics** (event-level user behavior), not web analytics (pageviews / traffic). The **EU region is required, not optional**: Pulse is NL-based, privacy-positioned (no ads, no surveillance, a calm space), launching to an EU audience, data stays in the EU and the GDPR story stays simple and honest.
- **Posture: instrument for learning, not surveillance.** Do **not** track-everything-figure-it-out-later, that is the surveillance default the positioning rejects, and a solo dev won't analyze undirected event noise anyway. Define a small, deliberate event set (~a dozen), each tied to a question already on the validation gate: **adaptive events firing** (ramp-back offered, deload triggered, behind/lapsed flagged); **override behavior** (deload followed vs dismissed, ramp-back accepted vs ignored, lighten-this-week used); **core-loop completion** (workout started vs finished, sets logged per session); **feel-coached surfaces** (Coach Timeline opened, adaptation explanations seen). Nothing beyond that set.
- **GDPR / consent is a hard requirement, the same legal floor as account-deletion + a privacy policy.** Behavioral analytics needs a truthful privacy-policy disclosure and a consent story (cookie/consent banner, with the privacy-preserving default selected). Analytics does not skip the legal floor; those legal pages are themselves launch-floor work.
- **Sequencing, the two-user validation gate does NOT need PostHog.** The gate is covered by the `DecisionEvent` log (Phase 0 #3 / Tier 1 #12), which captures the adaptive events as first-class rows in our own Supabase, no third party, no consent story for our own data, so validation runs off `DecisionEvent` + direct DB queries on our own training. PostHog is therefore deferred until real users exist whom you can't eyeball in the DB. Don't build it pre-launch.
- **Guard: analytics tells what, not why.** The deepest validation question, does completion-paced coaching actually keep someone progressing, is a judgment from watching real training, not a dashboard number. Instrument to *support* that judgment, never to replace it.

### Subsection 3: generation quality, ranking guard

#### 3.1 Isolation exercises can win a primary compound slot when the pool is thin

**Status:** Backlog (open, verified, with placement nuance and a golden-test risk)
**Track:** Generation quality
**Prerequisites:** 1.2 and 2.2 (they reduce how often this fallback is reached)
**Decision needed:** Exact sort placement and golden-test handling (see below). This is **not** purely additive.

**Root cause:** the `byPattern` comparator (`generation.ts:654-682`) never considers `is_compound`: its terms are demote, loading-lean, substitution-class freshness, front-delt suppression, fatigue, id. So within a pattern an isolation can sort above a compound (on freshness or fatigue) and be **selected** for a compound-pattern slot. `patternTier` (`:549-562`) only reorders **presentation** after selection, so it cannot stop an isolation from occupying the slot.
**Symptom:** under pool pressure (thin equipment, restrictions, or a pattern demanded more times than it has compounds) an isolation fills the session's main compound slot. The brief's "machine leg curl becomes the **primary movement**" is a selection problem, not a display one: `patternTier` already presents non-compound hinge/squat among the tier-4 finishers.
**Fix:** add an `is_compound` (compound-first) term to `byPattern`, placed **after** the front-delt-isolation term (`generation.ts:674-676`) and **before** the fatigue tiebreak (`:677-679`), so user-intent signals (loading lean, freshness) still win and fatigue stays the tiebreak. The brief's "only when filling the session's first compound-capable slot" is hard to express in the stateless per-pattern comparator; a simpler equivalent is a global compound-first term (a no-op for pure-isolation patterns, where all candidates share `is_compound = false`).
**Golden-test risk (flag):** this term **does** change which exercise is selected wherever an isolation currently out-sorts a compound, so it is not byte-identical. Either re-baseline the golden identity tests for the affected patterns, or scope the term to only break ties that today resolve isolation-over-compound. Verify before applying.
**Test stub:** fixture with one compound and one isolation for the same pattern; assert the compound is selected for the session's first compound-capable slot, and the isolation can still be selected for a subsequent same-pattern slot.
**Verified:** confirmed, no compound-first guard exists in `byPattern`/`pick`; added presentation-vs-selection nuance and the golden-test caveat.

Five confirmed generation-engine quality issues from systematic testing plus a three-way review (internal audit + ChatGPT + Perplexity), opened 2026-06-10 after the generation bug-fix work (#115 + the anchor/unilateral follow-ups). Confirmed against the live tree, not speculative. Distinct from subsections 1-3 above (those are the `EMPHASES` / metadata / ranking data fixes); this track is the next layer found after them. **Status (2026-06-10):** Items 1, 3, and Item 4's interim fix shipped on `feature/generation-engine-quality-track` (see each Status line). **Remaining:** Item 2 (the warning-surface decision must be made before build), Item 4's role model (spec-first), and Item 5 (spec-first, gated on Issue 0).

#### Item 1, Powerbuilding pull patterns: set/rep mismatch

**Status:** SHIPPED 2026-06-10 (`feature/generation-engine-quality-track`). `horizontal_pull` + `vertical_pull` added to `POWERBUILDING_HEAVY_PATTERNS`; the accessory unit test updated, +2 tests (heavy-pull unit + a PPL-pull end-to-end asserting 3-6 on the primary row). The "conservative pulls" variant remains a future per-pattern-bias-override option, not built.
**Problem:** `POWERBUILDING_HEAVY_PATTERNS` (squat / hinge / horizontal_push / vertical_push) excludes `horizontal_pull` and `vertical_pull`. Under powerbuilding a pull session gets the hypertrophy range (8-12) on every compound, but because `effectiveBias` resolved to `strength` the first compound still takes the strength +1 set bump. Net: the main row reads 4 sets × 8-12 and the back is never trained heavy under a style marketed as powerbuilding. An internal inconsistency, not a deliberate design choice.
**Fix:** add `horizontal_pull` and `vertical_pull` to `POWERBUILDING_HEAVY_PATTERNS`. Update the `repRange` / `resolveRepRange` unit tests and add an end-to-end test asserting a powerbuilding pull session produces 3-6 reps on the primary row compound.
**Note:** a "conservative pulls" variant (pulling stays moderate, 6-10, to manage joint stress) is a legitimate coaching philosophy, but it should be an explicit future option, not the silent default. Defer until the training-style system supports per-pattern bias overrides.
**Sequencing:** no dependencies; can ship in the next commit.

#### Item 2, Minimum-compound guard for restriction-emptied sessions

**Status:** Spec-ready, medium complexity. Does not require the full substitution system.
**Problem:** the restriction filter is purely subtractive with no floor. When combined restrictions empty a session's compound pool (e.g. knee + lower_back on a posterior lower day removes both the hinge and the lunge compounds), the engine silently generates an all-isolation session. A lower day of glute_iso + calf + core is a failure state, not a valid training session. knee + lower_back is common in real users and is the highest-severity untested combination in the current engine. (Generalizes the existing `lower_post` minimum-compound follow-up in the Status block, which covers only the equipment-emptied hinge case, to the restriction-driven case across all session types.)
**Fix:** after equipment + restriction filtering, before session construction, check the available compound count per session type. If compounds fall below a floor of 1 for any session, run a fallback search across all patterns in the safe pool (not just the session's emphasis patterns) for any available compound exercise; insert the first one found as the leading slot before backfill. If no compound exists after exhaustive search, generate the session anyway but surface a clear generation-time warning: "Your movement restrictions removed all compound options for one or more sessions. These sessions use accessory work only. Consider adjusting your restrictions or equipment." Do not hard-reject generation, an informed accessory-only session beats nothing.
**Dependencies:** uses existing `substitution_class` metadata (already seeded). Does not require the full substitution logic system.
**Follow-on:** a fuller pattern-aware fallback ranked by movement intent (a safe posterior compound when hinge is out, a safe quad compound when squat is out) is the next restrictions enhancement after this guard ships. Spec it separately as "movement restrictions v1.5, substitution logic."
**Sequencing:** no hard dependencies; can be implemented alongside Item 1.

#### Item 3, Remove `fb-emphasis-3` from the 3-day split options

**Status:** SHIPPED 2026-06-10 (`feature/generation-engine-quality-track`). `fb-emphasis-3` removed from `STYLES[3]` (now `fb-3` / `ppl-3` / `ulf-3`); the three orphaned emphases `fb_chest_back` / `fb_legs` / `fb_delts_arms` are kept as `EMPHASES` Record keys (removing them is a coupled `EmphasisKey` type change, and they are earmarked for the future replacement style). +1 test. The true "full-body with emphasis" replacement remains unbuilt (Future, below).
**Problem:** the 3-day style "Full Body - Emphasis Days" (`fb-emphasis-3`) is a body-part split that trains each muscle group roughly once per week: `fb_chest_back` (zero leg work), `fb_legs` (zero upper work), `fb_delts_arms` (minimal compound work). It appears alongside genuinely full-body 3-day options under a "Full Body" label; a user choosing it for full-body frequency gets the opposite, the lowest-frequency option in the catalog and inferior to every other 3-day option for the target user (intermediate lifter, hypertrophy + general strength).
**Fix:** remove `fb-emphasis-3` from `STYLES[3]`. The 3-day picker then offers three sound options: `fb-3` (full body, 3x frequency, recommended default), `ulf-3` (upper/lower/full body, ~1.5x frequency), and `ppl-3` (push/pull/legs, 1x frequency, clearly positioned for users who want specialization and understand the tradeoff).
**Future:** a true "full-body with emphasis" 3-day style, where each session trains all regions but one is prioritized, is the right long-term replacement for `fb-emphasis-3`. Spec it before building: every session must include at least one lower compound, one upper-push compound, and one upper-pull compound, with one pattern group front-loaded as the day's emphasis.
**Sequencing:** no dependencies. Remove now, spec the replacement separately.

#### Item 4, Squat/hinge adjacency: interim fix and long-term role model

**Status:** interim fix SHIPPED 2026-06-10 (`feature/generation-engine-quality-track`, `interleaveLowerCompounds` in `generation.ts`, applied after the tier sort, before supersets/set assignment; +3 tests including the set-bump-stays-on-position-0 interaction, code-reviewed by subagent, no blocking findings). The exercise-role model below remains a spec-first, architectural roadmap item.
**Problem:** the tier sort assigns both squat and hinge to Tier 1, guaranteeing they always appear as exercises 1 and 2 in any session containing both. Every full-body session, combined leg day, and PPL legs session opens with two maximally fatiguing lifts back to back, the single biggest coach-quality issue in the output. Established programming convention (5/3/1, GZCLP, most general-population templates) separates heavy lower compounds with an upper compound between them on combined sessions, or places squat first and hinge later as a secondary lift on dedicated leg days.
**Interim fix:** after the tier sort, run a post-sort pass. If exercises 1 and 2 are both Tier 1 lower-body compounds (squat and hinge patterns) and the session contains at least one Tier 2 upper compound, insert the first Tier 2 upper compound between them (squat → bench → RDL → row instead of squat → RDL → bench → row). On dedicated leg sessions with no Tier 2 upper compound to interleave (`lower_post`, `lower_quad`, PPL legs day) the adjacency is acceptable and no reorder fires, squat-then-hinge on a leg-only day is standard programming.
**Long-term direction, add as "Generation engine, exercise role model (architectural)":** replace or wrap the current pattern-tier model with an exercise-role layer, Primary Lower → Primary Upper → Secondary Lower → Secondary Upper → Isolation → Finisher. This encodes how coaches think about session structure, naturally separates heavy lower and upper compounds, and provides a clean hook for compound-floor validation (Item 2), rep-scheme assignment by role, and future volume-first generation. It is the same architectural conclusion reached independently by both external reviewers. Spec-first: do not build without a written spec defining role assignment rules for all 15 movement patterns and all session types. It gates the volume-first generation planner (Phase 4 in "Routine generation v2, engine direction" above; the source brief referenced it as Phase 3). The role-model spec should be written before that planner work begins.
**Sequencing:** the interim adjacency fix can ship with Items 1-3; the role-model spec is written before Phase 4 work begins.

#### Item 5, `ppl-x2-6` A/B session differentiation

**Status:** Spec-first. Do not implement until the day-picker redesign (Issue 0) ships, since the 6-day path is currently unreachable from the UI. (Issue 0 is not yet a standalone roadmap entry; it is tracked from the same testing/review session and should be filed separately.)
**Problem:** the 6-day PPL×2 style (`ppl-x2-6`) uses identical emphasis definitions for both push days, both pull days, and both leg days. Both leg days use the same slot list (squat, hinge, lunge, glute_iso, calf, core) with no quad/posterior differentiation and no heavy/volume contrast. Under `consistent` variety both leg days share the same squat and hinge anchors, producing near-identical sessions across the week, the 6-day equivalent of the cross-focus collapse already fixed in `ulppl-5`.
**Planned fix (spec-first):** apply the quad/posterior split `ulppl-5` already uses to the 6-day leg days, and add heavy/volume contrast to push and pull:
- Leg A: squat + lunge led (quad).
- Leg B: hinge + glute_iso led (posterior).
- Push A: bench-led, strength bias. Push B: OHP-led, hypertrophy or balanced bias.
- Pull A: horizontal-pull-compound emphasis, strength bias. Pull B: vertical-pull emphasis, hypertrophy or balanced bias.

The new session definitions (six emphasis definitions, the updated `STYLES[6]` entry, updated focus strings) must be written as a spec and reviewed before implementation; each new emphasis definition requires a passing golden test.
**Sequencing:** write the spec after Issue 0 ships (so the 6-day path is testable end-to-end); implement alongside or just after Issue 0.

## Competitive analysis (2026-06-03)

Reviewed 8 trackers: Hevy, Strong, Fitbod, Jefit, Boostcamp, Alpha Progression, Caliber, Setgraph. _Historical reference, all five "biggest gaps" below have since shipped; kept for the differentiation framing._

Biggest gaps versus the field:

- Live PR detection. Every serious tracker (Hevy, Boostcamp, Caliber, Strong) flags a personal record the moment you hit it. Pulse only surfaces PRs after the workout. Pulse already computes E1RM, so this is a small add with a big motivation payoff.
- Per-muscle weekly volume. Hevy, Fitbod, Jefit, Boostcamp, and Strong all show sets per muscle group per week, often as a body-diagram heat map. Pulse has a 10-category taxonomy and a volume-by-type chart already, so it can derive this from exercise tags.
- Plate calculator. Hevy, Strong, Boostcamp, Setgraph, and Caliber all ship one. It is a pure function with near-zero backend cost and removes real friction at the rack.
- Rich set types. Drop sets and failure sets are standard in Hevy, Strong, and Boostcamp. Pulse only has warmup and working sets.
- Mid-workout exercise swap. Boostcamp, Fitbod, Alpha, and Caliber let you swap a busy machine for a similar exercise and carry your weights across. High friction reducer for real gyms.

Differentiation opportunities:

- Recovery-aware volume nudges. Combine per-muscle volume with the existing RIR data to flag under- and over-trained muscles. Fitbod and Boostcamp do versions of this, but none pair it tightly with a fixed 12-week progressive-overload plan the way Pulse can.
- A single Strength Score. Caliber and Boostcamp prove a 0-100 composite metric drives engagement. Pulse already has E1RM PRs to compute it from, and it gives non-experts a legible headline number.
- Stay private and fast. Strong and Setgraph win on being distraction-free with no social noise. Pulse can lean into solo progressive overload as a positioning choice and skip the heavy social race that needs user mass.

## Strategic review (2026-06-06)
